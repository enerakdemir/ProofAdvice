How to request a supplier evidence pack

Importers and resellers often hold marketplace responsibility but not the design history. A structured evidence request reduces back-and-forth and makes gaps visible early. This guide is operational—not a substitute for your purchase contract or legal review of obligations.

  1. State your role and market. Clarify whether you are placing the product on the EU market, GB, or both, and whether you act as importer, distributor, or authorised representative—so the supplier knows which artefacts you need.
  2. Reference the exact SKU. Include model numbers, firmware version if relevant, and photos of rating plates.
  3. Ask for a bounded “evidence index”. Request a table listing document type, revision, scope (which variants), and language.
  4. Separate “summary” from “full file”. Start with test summaries and DoC; request full technical files under NDA if needed for deeper audits.
  5. Set deadlines in business days and name a single contact on your side to avoid fragmented replies.
  6. Log gaps explicitly. When something is missing, use a numbered gap list the supplier can respond to line-by-line (see our follow-up template).

If the supplier cannot produce coherent evidence for the configuration you sell, treat that as a sourcing risk, not a paperwork inconvenience.
