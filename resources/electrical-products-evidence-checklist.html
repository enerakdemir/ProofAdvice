Electrical consumer goods: evidence checklist

Electrical equipment often triggers both safety and EMC expectations. Harmonised standards and conformity procedures depend on product class and voltage—this page cannot classify your device. Use the list to ask better questions of your supplier and your test partner.

Design & ratings

  • Rated voltage, frequency, power, IP rating (if claimed), and intended climate / altitude if relevant.
  • Single SKU vs family variants—are test reports scoped to what you actually ship?

Test & design documentation

  • Summary of applicable tests (safety, EMC) with laboratory identity and dates.
  • Circuit/block diagrams, BOM critical safety parts, and software where it affects safety.
  • Change control if the product evolved after the last report.

Labels & consumer information

  • Artwork for rating plate, warnings, and disposal symbols (e.g. WEEE, battery).
  • Instruction manual aligned with the tested configuration and language markets.
