Common seller compliance mistakes

These patterns show up in triage calls across Amazon, Shopify, and hybrid B2B2C brands. Avoiding them does not automatically mean your product is compliant—but it reduces self-inflicted risk.

  1. Treating a supplier “CE certificate” as proof of everything. Ask what legislation and standards it references, whether it matches your SKU, and whether it is a test report, DoC, or marketing letter.
  2. Listing faster than the technical file updates. Firmware upgrades, bundle changes, or regional plugs can invalidate prior evidence silently.
  3. Wrong importer / manufacturer address on the label. Copy-paste errors between regions (EU vs UK) still generate enforcement cases.
  4. Soft-pedalling battery or radio modules. “Just Bluetooth” can still be material to RED / EMC scope and documentation.
  5. Assuming English-only instructions suffice for every EU market. Language rules vary by product and member state expectations.
  6. No owner for post-market surveillance inputs. Returns data and incident signals should feed back into conformity—especially for consumer electronics.
