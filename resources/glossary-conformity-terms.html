Glossary

Conformity terms sellers actually use

Short orientations for commercial teams. Not legal definitions; confirm critical terms with counsel or your conformity partner.

CE marking
A visible sign that the manufacturer (or other responsible party) declares conformity with applicable EU legislation for the product as placed on the market. It is not a “quality badge” from Amazon and does not replace technical evidence.
UKCA marking (Great Britain)
UK product marking for many goods placed on the market in Great Britain under UK rules. Requirements and transition timelines have evolved—check current UK government guidance for your product family.
Declaration of Conformity (DoC)
A formal statement identifying the product, legislation applied, standards or technical routes used, and the signatory. It should stay consistent with test reports and the physical product.
Test report
Laboratory output describing how a sample was tested and results against criteria. Scope (which models, configurations, voltages) matters more than the logo on the front page.
Labelling & markings
Text and symbols on the product or packaging: ratings, traceability, warnings, disposal, language requirements. Must match the technical file and the market you ship to.
Instruction manual
User-facing safety and assembly information. Must reflect the sold configuration and required languages; incomplete manuals are a frequent surveillance finding.
Importer
An economic operator placing goods from a third country on the EU market (and analogous UK concepts). Importers often must verify conformity documentation exists and keep copies—exact duties depend on product sector rules.
Authorised representative / Responsible person (GPSR etc.)
Roles that allow certain obligations to be held inside the EU or UK on behalf of a non-local manufacturer. Specific titles and duties vary by regulation—do not assume one appointment covers all product types.
