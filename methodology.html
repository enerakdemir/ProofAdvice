How CertPath works

CertPath is built for busy commercial teams: short structured intake, transparent rule-based outputs, optional AI wording that must stay aligned with those rules, and a clear path to human experts when stakes are high.

  1. 1

    Structured intake

    You answer a guided set of questions about the product, how you sell it (for example Amazon, Shopify, or your own store), your role in the supply chain, and basic risk signals such as electrical power, batteries, radio modules, skin contact, or children’s use. You also indicate target markets (UK and/or EU) and any documents you already hold.

    The intake is designed to be practical for SMEs—it does not replace a full technical file or test plan. Honest “not sure” answers are supported; the report surfaces assumptions where answers are incomplete.

  2. 2

    Rule-based logic (authoritative layer)

    A versioned deterministic rule engine maps your answers to structured outputs: likely compliance themes, suggested document types, gaps, risk flags, and suggested next steps. This layer is the reference for what the tool claims about your product—it is not invented ad hoc by a model.

    Rules encode general UK/EU marketplace-oriented triage patterns. They cannot capture every edge case or every sector-specific scheme. Where regulation depends on precise product design, classification, or economic operator role, expert validation is still required.

  3. 3

    Optional AI explanation support

    When enabled in a deployment, an AI model may generate plain-language explanations of the rule engine output—for example summarising gaps and next steps for a non-specialist reader. The model is instructed not to contradict the structured rule output or to invent new legal domains.

    In production-grade setups, AI calls should run server-side so provider keys are not exposed in browsers. Demo configurations may differ; see your deployment documentation.

  4. 4

    Official context (where configured)

    Some deployments enrich prompts with excerpts or references from selected public-sector guidance pages (for example high-level GOV.UK or EU portal material). References support tone and orientation; they do not automatically mean your product satisfies any cited page.

  5. 5

    Expert escalation

    CertPath is positioned as triage, not final sign-off. When the snapshot shows elevated risk, missing safety-related evidence, or ambiguous classification, you should engage qualified compliance consultants, testing laboratories, or legal advisers—especially before import, listing on regulated channels, or making claims to customers.

    The contact area of the site is intended to connect you with human support pathways appropriate to your deployment.

What CertPath does not do

  • Issue certificates or approve products for any market.
  • Replace notified bodies, accredited labs, or legal counsel.
  • Guarantee listing approval on Amazon or any other marketplace.
  • Collect or verify physical samples or full technical documentation unless a future product explicitly does so.
