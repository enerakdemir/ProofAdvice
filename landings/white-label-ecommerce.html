White-label e-commerce: know where conformity responsibility sits before you scale SKUs

You move fast on listings, bundles, and packaging refreshes—but economic operator roles and evidence chains do not speed up automatically. CertPath gives each priority SKU (or family) a repeatable intake and a snapshot of likely documentation themes, gaps, and risks so buying and ops can ask sharper questions of factories.

Pain points

Opaque factory packages

PDFs exist but do not map cleanly to the exact model or sales geography you white-label.

Retailer due diligence

B2B customers ask structured questionnaires your team answers ad hoc each time.

Rebranding risk

Logo and manual swaps can invalidate prior declarations if not managed with change control.

Portfolio sprawl

No shared vocabulary across merchandising, QA, and compliance on what “done” means per SKU.

Why this tool helps

  • Standardises the questions you should answer per product before you trust a factory pack.
  • Surfaces gaps and assumptions so commercial teams do not over-promise on listings.
  • Feeds directly into Starter Pack workflows when you need supplier templates and prep guidance at scale.

What the output includes

Rule-based lists: likely compliance themes, suggested document types, missing information flags, risk notes, and next steps—plus optional AI explanation constrained to that output. Not a legal opinion on who the importer must be or whether a factory relationship is sufficient.

Key risks for white-label operators

  • Assuming the factory’s DoC automatically transfers branding and address obligations to your entity.
  • Listing bundles where only one component in the kit has robust evidence.
  • Skipping re-validation when the underlying OEM design changes without notice.
