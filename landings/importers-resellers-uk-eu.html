Pain points

Split responsibilities

Unclear whether you are importer, distributor, or both for a given SKU and geography.

Last-mile listing pressure

Marketplaces demand answers faster than your supplier’s engineering team responds.

Evidence in foreign languages

You receive adequate technical content but cannot map it to your English listing claims.

Cash-constrained triage

You need to decide where to spend on retests or counsel first—without a shared internal snapshot.

Why CertPath helps

  • Captures seller role and markets explicitly—inputs that change what you should expect to hold.
  • Produces a repeatable snapshot you can attach to internal tickets or forward to advisers.
  • Connects to supplier follow-up templates and Expert Review when stakes rise.

What the output includes

Likely document and conformity themes, missing-information checklist, risk flags, and prioritised next steps from the rule engine; optional AI explanation. Does not determine your legal status as importer or your obligations under specific regulations—that requires professional review.

Key risks for this segment

  • Signing marketplace agreements without matching conformity file access.
  • Assuming the manufacturer’s address on the product satisfies your market’s economic operator expectations.
  • Under-documenting traceability when mixing batches from multiple factories.

FAQ

Does CertPath tell me if I am the importer?

No. It captures the role you select and reflects it in the snapshot. Legal classification of economic operators needs counsel.

Can I use this in customs disputes?

No. It is internal triage. Authorities expect formal evidence and declarations.

How do I escalate?

Use packages to compare Starter Pack vs Expert Review, or contact directly.
