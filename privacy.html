Privacy Policy

Effective date: 18 April 2026 · Questions: hello@certpath.com

1. Who this notice covers

This Privacy Policy describes how information is handled when you use the CertPath website and assessment experience (the “Service”). The Service is operated for the purpose of product compliance triage and decision support. It is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice.

2. What information you may provide

Depending on how you use the Service, you may provide or generate:

  • Product and business intake. Fields such as product name, listing URL, sales channel, product category, seller role, risk-related answers, target markets, and documents you already hold. This is used to produce a structured triage snapshot.
  • Optional AI explanation. If you use a server-side AI endpoint, a subset of your intake and the output of the built-in rule engine may be sent to that endpoint to generate plain-language explanation text. No separate “legal review” is performed by the model.
  • Contact details. If you use a contact or enquiry form, you may provide name, email, company, phone, and a free-text message. In the current static deployment, typical browser demos may not transmit this data to a CertPath server; treat any demo form as non-production unless a backend is explicitly configured.

3. Where data is stored and processed

  • Browser and device. To improve usability, the Service may store assessment drafts, language preference, optional report history, and similar items in your browser (for example using localStorage). This stays on your device unless you clear it or use a feature that explicitly sends data elsewhere.
  • Server-side AI (when enabled). If an AI endpoint is configured, requests are sent over HTTPS to that endpoint. The operator of the endpoint should apply appropriate technical and organisational measures (including contractual terms with subprocessors such as model providers). You should review their documentation before use in production.
  • No certification decisions. The Service does not replace notified bodies, accredited testing laboratories, or regulators. Outputs are preliminary guidance only.

4. Purposes of processing

Information is used to: run the structured assessment; display results; optionally request AI-generated explanations aligned with the rule engine; maintain session convenience on your device; and, where a real backend exists, respond to enquiries and improve the Service in line with applicable law.

5. Legal bases (UK / EEA context)

Where UK GDPR or EU GDPR applies, typical bases include: performance of a contract or steps prior to contracting (where applicable); legitimate interests in operating and securing a B2B triage tool, balanced against your rights; and consent where required (for example for non-essential cookies or marketing, if offered). The precise basis depends on deployment context—production operators should document this in their records of processing.

6. Retention

Browser-stored data persists until you clear site data or use in-app reset features. Server-side retention (if any) should be defined by the deployment operator—for example minimum retention for abuse prevention versus deletion on request.

7. Sharing and international transfers

Data may be shared with: infrastructure and AI providers strictly as needed to operate the Service; professional advisers under confidentiality; or authorities where required by law. Transfers outside the UK/EEA should rely on appropriate safeguards (such as UK IDTA / EU SCCs) where applicable.

8. Your rights

Subject to jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, rectify, erase, restrict, or object to certain processing, and to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Contact hello@certpath.com for privacy requests. You can also clear local browser storage at any time from your browser settings.

9. Security

Reasonable technical measures such as HTTPS should be used in production deployments. No online service can be guaranteed perfectly secure; avoid submitting highly sensitive personal data unless a deployment explicitly supports and secures it.

10. Children

The Service is intended for businesses and adult users. It is not directed at children for the collection of their personal data.

11. Changes

This policy may be updated to reflect product or legal changes. The effective date at the top will be revised when material updates are made.
