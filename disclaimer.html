Important limitations

Read this page carefully before relying on any CertPath output for commercial, regulatory, or safety-critical decisions.

Preliminary guidance only

CertPath provides preliminary, informational guidance based on the answers you supply and the tool’s built-in logic. It is designed as a triage aid for internal planning and conversations with advisers—not as a final determination of regulatory status, conformity, or listing eligibility.

Not legal advice

Nothing on this site or in generated reports constitutes legal advice or a solicitor–client relationship. Statutes, delegated acts, harmonised standards, guidance documents, and marketplace policies change and may be interpreted differently in specific cases. You should obtain advice from qualified legal counsel where appropriate.

Not formal certification or accredited testing

CertPath does not perform conformity assessment, issue certificates of conformity, or carry out product testing. It is not a substitute for accredited laboratories, notified bodies (where required), or official registration processes. Any reference to “certificates” in the product refers to types of evidence commonly discussed in UK/EU trade, not to a claim that you hold or need a specific named certificate unless you separately obtain it through proper channels.

AI-assisted outputs

Where AI explanations are enabled, text is generated by statistical models. Models can omit nuance, reflect training cut-offs, or occasionally phrase content imperfectly even when constrained by rules. Always cross-check AI wording against the structured rule output, your technical documentation, and professional advisers.

Your facts and evidence matter

Final suitability for any market depends on the actual product (design, materials, ratings, markings), complete technical evidence, supplier declarations, test reports, and how the product is presented and sold. Incomplete or inaccurate intake answers will produce incomplete or inaccurate triage.

Marketplaces and authorities

Amazon, eBay, customs authorities, and regulators apply their own checks and enforcement. CertPath does not guarantee compliance with any platform policy or government programme.

Expert validation

Where the snapshot shows elevated risk, missing safety-related documentation, uncertainty about classification, or contradictions in your answers, you should stop and engage qualified experts before listing, importing, or making public claims about compliance.
